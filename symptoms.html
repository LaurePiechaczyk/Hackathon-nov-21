SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS

Statistics

Almost half (47%) of the female population of the United Kingdom do not check their breasts for signs of cancer on a regular basis.

One in 10 have never checked them. Research tells us that one in 7 women will develop breast cancer in their lifetime.
With high figures like this it is important to spread the message that everyone (men included) need to incorporate a regular check into our busy schedules.

ONE DIAGNOSIS EVERY 10 MINS

What are the early signs and symptoms of breast cancer?


LUMP OR SWELLING

Check the breast, armpits and upper cheast area for lumps/swelling. These may not be visible so check by touch.

COLOUR

A change in the color of the breast - the area may look inflamed or red.

SIZE AND SHAPE

A change in the size and shape of the breast.

RASH

Any crusting, rash or changes to the nipple.

UNUSUAL DISCHARGE

Any unusual discharge from either nipple.



STATISTICS

As of 2021 breast cancer has become the most common cancer in women in the UK

Around 55,000 WOMEN and 370 MEN are diagnosed with breast cancer every year. This amounts to one diagnosis every 10 minutes.

The good news is that thanks to the advancements in medicine and early dectection the survival rate is always improving. It has doubled in the past 40 years and continues to rise.
But early detection is the best weapon against the spread of breast cancer.

UNAVOIDABLE RISKS
